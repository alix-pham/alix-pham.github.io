### Templates I made

* [Weighted Factor Model](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1Og3uowW4c0og6_Ce9yjmBWuKvWTMvN2-CthhxFLEgp4/edit?gid=1235323028#gid=1235323028) — MCDA template to quantitatively score options and help you make decisions; inspired by [this post](https://www.charityentrepreneurship.com/post/weighted-factor-models-five-examples-using-a-spreadsheet-to-make-good-decisions)

### Questions I like to ask people

*You can replace "work" or "job" with "life" or any container for parts of people's lives.*

* What are the best and worst parts about your job?
* What are the main misconceptions in your field?
* What are one barrier and one opportunity in your unique context?
* What is the strongest disagreement you have with your colleagues or peers?
* What most defies common sense in your work?
* What are the most underestimated opportunities in your field?
* If you could change just one thing in your job or life, what would it be?
* What is your biggest uncertainty in your work?
* What assumption about your work proved most wrong recently?
* What is a wild idea you haven't shared yet?
* What are you most and least proud of?
* Who should I talk to?

### Opening lines

My quick guide to starting conversations with strangers:

* **Lawful good:** I'd really like to chat about \<x\> with you
* **Neutral good:** I really like your outfit!
* **Chaotic good:** Someone told me to talk to you, but I don't remember who
* **Lawful neutral:** I don't think we've met, I'm \<y\>
* **Neutral neutral:** Hi!
* **Chaotic neutral:** Have we met?
* **Lawful evil:** I'm sorry, who are you?
* **Neutral evil:** I'm pretty sure we met somewhere
* **Chaotic evil:** Are you \<z\>? I'm looking for \<z\> *(while you know they're not)*
